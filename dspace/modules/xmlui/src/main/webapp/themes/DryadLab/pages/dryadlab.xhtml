DryadLab is an educational complement to the Dryad repository. Here you will find classroom activities that make use of research data archived in Dryad.

DryadLab is still in development. Please let us know if you find any errors in this content.

Current Activities

A walk through the woods: data analysis of structural adaptations in wood

  • Overview: Students will 1) form hypotheses about the influence of environmental variables on wood structure, 2) test their hypotheses using wood structure data, 3) interpret their results in light of environmental pressures and physiological function and 4) revise hypotheses as necessary.
  • Keywords: hypothesis formulation, hypothesis testing, data visualization, graphing, adaptation, environmental pressures, wood structure and function, ecology, botany, forestry, climate change
  • Instruction Level: Introductory and advanced undergraduate biology courses, high school AP biology
  • Duration: 30 minutes + homework

Survivorship in the natural world

  • Overview: Real-life data from seven wild primate populations provide examples of real survivorship curves.
  • Keywords: survivorship, life history, population ecology, demography, life tables, age structure
  • Instruction Level: Undergraduate (Intro Biology or Intro Ecology), AP Biology
  • Duration: Flexible: 20 minutes - 2.5 hours

Introduction to extinctions and extinction bias

  • Overview: Students will be introduced to different factors that might affect extinction risk and will work with data on artiodactyls (even-toed, hoofed mammals, such as cows, sheep, deer, antelope, and giraffes) to investigate some of these hypotheses.
  • Keywords: extinction, data analysis, graphing, macroevolution, ecology, conservation
  • Instruction Level: high school, AP high school, intro level undergrad biology
  • Duration: 1.5 hours OR 15 minutes + homework
